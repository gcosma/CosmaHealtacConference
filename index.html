Prevention of Future Death Reports
What are PFD Reports?
Independent judicial assessments issued by coroners following inquests into unexpected deaths, providing cross-organisational perspectives on patient safety issues.

Research Challenge: Despite UK government goals to reduce maternal deaths by 50% by 2025, maternal mortality rates increased by 3% between 2010-2012 and 2018-2020 (excluding COVID-19 deaths).

Research Gap: Maternal and neonatal deaths have not been systematically analysed across PFD reports despite persistent disparities in maternal mortality.
51 PFD Reports
UK Judiciary Data
2014-2025 Study Period

Data Collection Method: Specialised web scraping tool extracted PFD reports from UK Judiciary website using maternal healthcare search terms: Midwifery, Birth, Baby, Maternal, Infant, Obstetrics, Neonatal, Perinatal, Pregnancy, Postnatal, Antenatal, Maternity, Stillbirth, Antepartum, Fetal/Foetal.

Framework Development

Three Complementary Frameworks:

1. Serious Incident Research Framework (I-SIRch): Sociotechnical categories developed for maternity safety analysis
2. Black Maternal Health Framework: Equity dimensions extracted from House of Commons Committee report on Black maternal health
3. Extended Safety Framework: Emerging themes including structural inequities and digital access barriers from textual analysis
Triangulation Approach: Multiple frameworks enable comprehensive thematic coverage while revealing framework-specific blind spots in safety concerns identification.
I-SIRch Framework Categories: Person factors, Job/Task factors, Organisation factors, Technologies & Tools, Environment factors.
Framework Integration: Simultaneous analysis across all three frameworks ensures comprehensive coverage of safety factors while maintaining analytical rigor.

Conclusions & Impact
Clinical Implications: Judicial assessments reveal distinct safety priorities focused on legal accountability and patient rights, providing unique insights complementary to traditional incident investigation approaches.
Model Performance Insights: Our refined ClinicalBERT model showed improved performance in identifying clinical themes like Medication Safety but had limitations with broader systemic factors. The triangulation approach allows comprehensive thematic coverage while revealing framework-specific blindspots in safety concerns identification.
Future Directions: Systematic PFD analysis can inform targeted interventions for medication safety protocols and informed consent processes in maternity care.

Study Limitations: Ethnicity data was not available in the PFD reports, preventing evaluation of theme performance across different ethnic groups.
